The Four Files Guide

One of the first things every UNIUYO fresher hears about is the "Four Files." This guide explains what they are, why they are required, and the documents you should arrange inside them.

Read Guide

What Are the Four Files?

The Four Files are folders used to organize your important admission and registration documents. Different university offices may request different copies during screening and clearance, so having your documents properly arranged saves time and prevents unnecessary stress.

Why Are They Important?

  • They keep your documents organized.
  • They make screening faster.
  • You can easily submit documents when requested.
  • They help prevent loss or damage to important papers.

Documents to Include

Prepare several photocopies of each document and keep the originals safe.

  • JAMB Admission Letter
  • JAMB Result Slip
  • Acceptance Fee Receipt
  • School Fees Receipt
  • Course Registration Form
  • Student Biodata Form
  • O'Level Result (WAEC/NECO/NABTEB)
  • Birth Certificate or Age Declaration
  • Certificate of Local Government of Origin
  • Passport Photographs
  • NIN Slip (if requested)
  • Medical Clearance Documents (if applicable)

How to Arrange Your Documents

  1. Place your original documents in a separate safe folder.
  2. Arrange photocopies neatly inside each file.
  3. Keep documents in the same order across all files.
  4. Label each file with your full name, department, registration number, and phone number.
  5. Avoid folding important documents.

Important Tips

  • Make at least five photocopies of every document.
  • Print multiple copies of every payment receipt.
  • Use clear and readable photocopies.
  • Keep digital copies on your phone or cloud storage.
  • Always carry a few extra passport photographs.

Things to Avoid

  • Do not submit original documents unless specifically requested.
  • Do not mix originals with photocopies.
  • Do not wait until your screening day to arrange your files.
  • Do not leave your file unlabeled.

Important Notice

The exact number of files, file colors, and required documents may differ depending on your department or the current academic session. Always confirm the latest instructions from the Department of Data Science or the Faculty of Computing before your screening date.
